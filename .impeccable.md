# Nord Axis Tech — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Dual audience**: Industrial managers and C-suite decision-makers (plant directors, CTOs, VP Engineering at manufacturing companies) AND technical evaluators (automation engineers, maintenance leads). Both visit the site — executives need to feel confidence and prestige, engineers need to feel technical credibility. The language must be precise and free of fluff. Both groups are experienced professionals who can spot superficial design immediately.

### Brand Personality
**Precise & Authoritative** — like a top-tier engineering consultancy. Confident, data-driven, no marketing speak. The site should feel like it was built by people who actually understand Industry 4.0, not by a creative agency with a stock photo library.

Three words: **Precise. Expert. Trustworthy.**

Emotional goals: Trust & credibility (this company knows what they're doing), Forward-thinking (ahead of the curve), Accessible & approachable (they understand our problems), Premium/elite (not a budget option).

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference**: Siemens / ABB style — clean, professional, enterprise industrial. Structured, authoritative, light backgrounds, strong typographic hierarchy, restrained use of brand color.

**Anti-references**:
- Gaming / military HUD aesthetic (tactical overlays, system-status tags, neon-on-dark)
- Developer dark mode with glowing terminal vibes
- Startup marketing fluff (gradients, glassmorphism, hero metrics with gradient text)
- Generic B2B SaaS (card grids everywhere, identical icon + heading + text blocks)

**Theme**: Light — off-white to light gray backgrounds, very dark (near-black) text, teal/cyan accent used sparingly and purposefully.

**Typography**: Bebas Neue for high-impact display headings only. DM Sans 400/500 for body and UI. Space Mono only for data labels and genuine numeric readouts — not decorative "HUD" text.

**Color**: Teal (#00A896) accent on light backgrounds. Dark navy/slate for text. Subtle warm-gray surfaces. No neon glow effects.

### Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration** — Every element must serve a purpose. Remove anything that is purely decorative without functional meaning. If a corner bracket or system tag doesn't convey information, it's noise.

2. **Authority through restraint** — Enterprise buyers trust quiet confidence more than flashy design. Whitespace, clean typography, and controlled hierarchy communicate expertise better than visual complexity.

3. **Technical credibility, human warmth** — The site targets both C-suite and engineers. Speak with precision but avoid cold, clinical sterility. The brand is an expert advisor, not a machine.

4. **Structured, not rigid** — Use a clear grid and visual hierarchy but allow intentional asymmetry at key moments (hero, CTA). Siemens/ABB sites feel organized without feeling bureaucratic.

5. **Accent means accent** — Teal is used to mark what matters: primary actions, key data points, active states. It should never be used decoratively or repeatedly enough that it loses meaning.
